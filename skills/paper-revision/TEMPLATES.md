# Response Templates

Ready-to-use templates for academic paper revision responses.

---

## Response Letter Structure

### Overall Document Template

```markdown
# Response to Reviewers

**Manuscript ID**: [ID]
**Manuscript Title**: [Title]
**Journal**: [Journal Name]
**Revision Round**: [1/2/3]
**Date**: [Date]

---

Dear Editor and Reviewers,

We sincerely thank you for the opportunity to revise our manuscript and for the constructive feedback provided. We have carefully addressed all comments and made comprehensive revisions to strengthen our work.

Below, we provide point-by-point responses to each comment. All modifications in the manuscript are highlighted in **yellow** for your convenience.

---

## RESPONSE TO REVIEWER [ID]

[Per-comment responses here]

---

## RESPONSE TO EDITORIAL COMMENTS

[Editorial responses here]

---

We believe these revisions have substantially improved the manuscript. We thank the reviewers for their valuable insights and hope the revised version meets the journal's standards for publication.

Sincerely,
[Author Names]
```

## Phrase Library

### Acknowledgment Phrases

**Positive acknowledgment**:
- "We thank the reviewer for this insightful observation..."
- "We appreciate this constructive suggestion..."
- "We are grateful for this valuable feedback..."
- "Thank you for highlighting this important point..."

**Acknowledging oversight**:
- "We apologize for this oversight in the original manuscript..."
- "We acknowledge that the original description was insufficient..."
- "We recognize that this section required additional clarity..."

### Modification Description Phrases

**For additions**:
- "We have added a new paragraph explaining..."
- "We have expanded this section to include..."
- "A new subsection has been added to address..."

**For revisions**:
- "We have revised this paragraph to clarify..."
- "This section has been substantially rewritten to..."
- "We have modified the wording to more accurately reflect..."

**For corrections**:
- "We have corrected the calculation error..."
- "The values have been updated to reflect..."
- "We have fixed the inconsistency by..."

### Softening Language

**For overclaiming**:
- "demonstrates" → "suggests"
- "proves" → "provides evidence for"
- "clearly shows" → "indicates"
- "will" → "may"
- "establishes" → "supports"

**For limitations**:
- "This finding may not generalize to..."
- "Future research should examine..."
- "While our study has limitations, including..."
- "Interpretation should consider that..."

### Location Citation Phrases

- "As shown in the revised text (Methods, Paragraph 16)..."
- "We have updated the Discussion section (Paragraphs 45-47)..."
- "See the revised Table 3 for..."
- "The new Figure S2 illustrates..."

## Journal-Specific Formats

### JMIR Family Journals

```
P-value format: P<.05 (no leading zero, capital P)
Citation format: [1]
Supplementary: Multimedia Appendix 1
Tables in appendix: Table S1, Table S2
Abstract sections: Background, Objective, Methods, Results, Conclusions
```

### Nature Family Journals

```
P-value format: P < 0.05 (spaces, capital P)
Citation format: superscript numbers¹²
Supplementary: Extended Data Fig. 1, Supplementary Table 1
Methods: Usually after References
Abstract: Single paragraph, no sections
```

### Lancet Family Journals

```
P-value format: p<0·05 (lowercase p, middle dot)
Citation format: superscript numbers
Supplementary: Appendix (pp 1-10)
Methods: Panel format for certain elements
Abstract: Structured with specific sections
```

### PLOS Family Journals

```
P-value format: p < 0.05 (lowercase p)
Citation format: [1]
Supplementary: S1 Table, S1 Fig
Data availability: Required section
Abstract: Single paragraph
```
